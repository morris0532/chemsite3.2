## 1. REACH Registration: The Gateway to the European Market

For any buyer importing Soda Ash into the European Economic Area (EEA), **REACH (Registration, Evaluation, Authorisation and Restriction of Chemicals)** is the most critical hurdle.

### The Requirement:
Under REACH, any manufacturer or importer of Soda Ash in quantities of 1 ton or more per year must register the substance with the **European Chemicals Agency (ECHA)**. Soda Ash is registered under **EC Number 207-838-8**.

### The Strategic Advantage:
China's leading Soda Ash exporters, like **Sinopeakchem**, maintain full REACH registration through their **Only Representative (OR)** in Europe. This allows EU buyers to import material without the burden of performing their own registration, significantly simplifying the procurement process and ensuring full legal compliance.

## 2. ISO Standards: The Benchmark of Quality and Sustainability

ISO certifications are the "universal language" of industrial excellence. When auditing a China Soda Ash factory, two standards are non-negotiable:

### ISO 9001:2015 (Quality Management)
This standard ensures that the factory has a robust system for monitoring every stage of production, from raw material (brine/limestone) intake to final bagging. It guarantees that the **Purity (≥ 99.2%)** and **PSD (Particle Size Distribution)** you receive in January will be identical to what you receive in December.

### ISO 14001:2015 (Environmental Management)
In the context of China's strict environmental enforcement, ISO 14001 is a risk-management tool. A factory with this certification is less likely to face sudden government-mandated shutdowns due to pollution violations, ensuring a more stable and predictable supply for your plant.

---

## 3. SDS and GHS: Ensuring Safe Handling and Transport

The **Safety Data Sheet (SDS)** is the most important technical document accompanying a Soda Ash shipment. Under the **Globally Harmonized System (GHS)**, a compliant SDS must contain 16 standardized sections.

### Key Sections for Procurement:
*   **Section 2 (Hazard Identification)**: Soda Ash is classified as an eye irritant (Category 2).
*   **Section 9 (Physical and Chemical Properties)**: Provides the definitive data on solubility, density, and pH.
*   **Section 14 (Transport Information)**: Confirms that Soda Ash is **Not Regulated** as a dangerous good for sea (IMDG) or air (IATA) transport, which simplifies logistics and reduces freight costs.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### 1. Is Soda Ash considered a "Hazardous Chemical" for shipping?
No. Under GHS and international transport regulations (IMDG/IATA), Soda Ash is not classified as a hazardous material for transport. However, it is an eye irritant and requires proper PPE during handling.

### 2. What is an "Only Representative" (OR) in REACH?
An OR is a natural or legal person established in the EU who acts on behalf of a non-EU manufacturer to fulfill the REACH registration obligations.

### 3. How do I know if a China factory's ISO certificate is authentic?
You can verify the certificate number on the website of the **CNAS (China National Accreditation Service)** or the specific international registrar (e.g., SGS, TUV, or BSI).

### 4. Does Soda Ash require a "Certificate of Origin"?
Yes. For many countries, a Certificate of Origin (CO) is required to determine the applicable import duties and to benefit from Free Trade Agreements (FTAs).

### 5. What is the difference between Technical Grade and Food Grade compliance?
Food Grade Soda Ash (E500i) must meet much stricter limits for heavy metals and must be produced under **HACCP or ISO 22000** food safety management systems.
