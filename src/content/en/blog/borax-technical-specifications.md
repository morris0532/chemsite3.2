## 1. Core Chemical Properties
Understanding the fundamental chemistry of Borax is essential for predicting its behavior in industrial formulations.

### 1.1 Molecular Structure
Borax is a salt of boric acid. In its crystalline form, it consists of tetraborate ions [B₄O₅(OH)₄]²⁻ linked by sodium ions and water molecules. This structure is responsible for its excellent buffering and fluxing capabilities.

### 1.2 pH and Buffering Capacity
Borax is a mild alkaline salt. A 1% aqueous solution typically has a pH of approximately **9.24 at 20°C**. It is one of the most effective pH buffers available, resisting changes in acidity or alkalinity, which is critical for:
- Enzyme stability in liquid detergents
- Corrosion inhibition in metalworking fluids and antifreeze

### 1.3 Solubility Dynamics
Borax solubility is highly temperature-dependent, a property leveraged in crystallization and purification processes.
- At 20°C: ~4.7 g/100ml water
- At 100°C: ~190 g/100ml water

### 1.4 Thermal Behavior and Hydration States
The various hydration states of Borax are defined by water content and thermal stability.

- **Decahydrate (Na₂B₄O₇·10H₂O)**: Loses water at ~75°C, fully dehydrated at 320°C.
- **Pentahydrate (Na₂B₄O₇·5H₂O)**: Loses water at ~200°C, fully dehydrated at 400°C.
- **Anhydrous (Na₂B₄O₇)**: No water, stable to 743°C, ideal for high-temperature processes.

## 2. Borax Grades and Applications
Borax is available in multiple grades tailored to industrial, agricultural, and high-purity demands.

### 2.1 Technical Grade
Most widely used for general industry.
- Purity: 99.0%–99.9%
- Applications: Glass, detergents, metallurgy, adhesives, fire retardants

### 2.2 Agricultural Grade
Optimized for plant nutrition.
- High solubility for foliar and liquid fertilizers
- Controlled particle size for blending with NPK
- Low heavy metals to avoid phytotoxicity

### 2.3 High-Purity / ACS Reagent Grade
For labs, pharma, optics, and electronics.
- Purity: ≥99.95%
- Ultra-low heavy metals and insoluble impurities

### 2.4 Specialty Grades
- Nuclear Grade: Neutron absorption with controlled B-10 isotopes
- Food Grade: For indirect food contact applications

## 3. Critical Impurity Limits (COA Reference)
When reviewing a Certificate of Analysis, these impurities directly impact product performance.

| Impurity | Typical Limit (Tech Grade) | Impact |
| :------- | :------------------------ | :----- |
| Iron (Fe) | < 10–50 ppm | Causes tint in glass/ceramics |
| Sulfate (SO₄) | < 100–500 ppm | Interferes with chemical reactions |
| Chloride (Cl) | < 50–200 ppm | Accelerates corrosion |
| Heavy Metals (Pb, As, Cd) | < 1–10 ppm | Toxicity risk for agri & personal care |
| Insolubles | < 0.05–0.1% | Clogs nozzles, causes defects |
| Ca & Mg | < 100–500 ppm | Reduces detergent efficiency |

## 4. Physical Specifications
Physical properties directly affect handling, automation, and process efficiency.

### 4.1 Particle Size Distribution
- **Granular (15–100 mesh)**: For silos, auto‑batching, glass, fertilizer blending
- **Powdered**: For fast dissolution (detergents, sprays)
- **Coarse**: For slow dissolution or metallurgy

### 4.2 Bulk Density
- Decahydrate: ~0.85 g/cm³
- Pentahydrate: ~1.0 g/cm³
- Anhydrous: ~1.2 g/cm³

### 4.3 Flowability
Pentahydrate offers the best flowability and anti-caking performance for automated systems.

## 5. Quality Control and Certifications
### 5.1 Certificate of Analysis (COA)
Every batch must include:
- B₂O₃ and Na₂O content
- Impurity levels (Fe, Cl, SO₄, heavy metals)
- Particle size, moisture, insolubles
- Batch number, production date

### 5.2 Global Certifications
- ISO 9001 (Quality)
- ISO 14001 (Environment)
- REACH (EU)
- TSCA (US)
- GMP (Pharma/Cosmetics)
- Kosher / Halal (as required)
