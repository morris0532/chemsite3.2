## 1. Chemical and Physical Properties
Borax Decahydrate is a white, odorless, crystalline solid. Its technical performance is defined by its specific molecular structure and hydration state.

### 1.1 Technical Specifications
| Property | Value/Description |
| :------- | :---------------- |
| **Chemical Formula** | Na₂B₄O₇·10H₂O |
| **Molecular Weight** | 381.37 g/mol |
| **B₂O₃ Content (Theoretical)** | 36.5% |
| **Na₂O Content (Theoretical)** | 16.2% |
| **pH (1% solution @ 20°C)** | ~9.2 |
| **Solubility in Water (g/100ml)** | 4.7 (20°C), 190 (100°C) |
| **Specific Gravity** | 1.73 |
| **Melting Point** | 75°C (decomposes with loss of water) |
| **Appearance** | White, crystalline solid |
| **CAS Number** | 1303-96-4 |

### 1.2 The Role of Water of Crystallization
The "Decahydrate" designation refers to the ten molecules of water chemically bound to the sodium tetraborate structure. This high water content makes it the most cost-effective form of Borax to produce, as it requires the least amount of energy for dehydration compared to Pentahydrate or Anhydrous grades. However, it also means higher logistics costs per unit of active B₂O₃.

### 1.3 Crystalline Structure and Stability
Borax Decahydrate crystallizes in the monoclinic system. Its crystal structure is crucial for its physical properties, including its relatively low hardness and good solubility. While stable under normal conditions, prolonged exposure to dry air can lead to efflorescence (loss of water of crystallization), gradually converting it to Borax Pentahydrate or even Anhydrous Borax. Conversely, exposure to high humidity can lead to caking due to moisture absorption.

## 2. Primary Industrial Applications: The Versatility of Decahydrate
Borax Decahydrate is the "workhorse" of the borate family, valued for its high solubility, mild alkalinity, and buffering capacity. These properties make it indispensable across a wide range of industrial applications.

### 2.1 Detergents, Cleaning Products, and Personal Care
Borax Decahydrate has been a staple in cleaning formulations for over a century due to its multi-functional benefits:
- **pH Buffering**: Maintains stable alkaline environment (pH ~9.2)
- **Water Softening**: Sequesters calcium and magnesium ions
- **Enzyme Stabilization**: Protects enzymes in liquid detergents
- **Mild Antiseptic/Antifungal**: Inhibits mold and mildew
- **Cosmetics**: Used as emulsifier, buffer, and preservative

### 2.2 Agriculture: A Soluble Source of Boron Micronutrient
Borax Decahydrate is the preferred boron source for fast-acting agricultural applications:
- **Foliar Fertilizers**: Rapid dissolution for quick absorption
- **Liquid Fertilizers & Fertigation**: Easy mixing in irrigation systems
- **Soil Application**: Compatible with NPK blending

### 2.3 Metallurgy and Fluxing
Acts as a reliable flux for low-temperature brazing and soldering:
- Dissolves surface oxides
- Protects metals from re-oxidation
- Ensures strong, clean metal joints

### 2.4 Corrosion Inhibition
Widely used in cooling systems and metalworking fluids:
- Forms protective film on ferrous surfaces
- Prevents rust, pitting, and scale buildup
- Standard component in antifreeze & coolants

### 2.5 Adhesives and Sealants
Used in starch-based adhesives for corrugated cardboard and paper lamination:
- Improves tack and bonding strength
- Enhances water resistance of finished glue

## 3. B2B Pricing and Procurement Factors
Procuring Borax Decahydrate requires evaluating total cost beyond unit price.

### 3.1 Logistics Efficiency: The "Water Factor"
With ~47% water content, Decahydrate is less freight-efficient for long distances. It is most cost-effective for local buyers or applications requiring high solubility.

### 3.2 Quality Grades
- **Technical Grade**: For general industry
- **Agricultural Grade**: Particle-size standardized for fertilizers
- **Specialty Grades**: Ultra-low impurity for pharma & electronics

### 3.3 Market Volatility
Price drivers include:
- Energy costs for production
- Global supply concentration (Turkey & USA)
- Industry demand (glass, agriculture, detergents)
- Currency exchange rates

## 4. Handling and Storage
Borax Decahydrate requires careful storage to avoid caking and degradation.

### 4.1 Caking & Moisture Control
- Store in cool, dry, ventilated warehouses
- Use moisture-barrier packaging for sea freight
- Avoid humidity and temperature swings

### 4.2 Inventory Management
- Strict FIFO policy
- Palletize off the floor
- Avoid overstacking bags

### 4.3 Safety & PPE
- Eye protection
- Gloves for skin protection
- Respiratory protection in dusty areas
- Adequate ventilation
