## 2. Detailed Product Specifications: The Blueprint for Quality

Precision in chemical procurement is non-negotiable, and it begins with clear, accurate, and verifiable product specifications. Our detailed product specifications documents provide the exact chemical and physical properties of our Sodium Thiosulphate, serving as the definitive blueprint to ensure that the product you receive precisely matches your stringent requirements [2].

### Key Specification Parameters Typically Included:

*   **Chemical Formula**: Clearly states Na₂S₂O₃·5H₂O (Pentahydrate) or Na₂S₂O₃ (Anhydrous).
*   **Purity/Assay**: Specifies the minimum percentage of Sodium Thiosulphate, typically ≥99.0% for pentahydrate and ≥98.0% for anhydrous, ensuring chemical efficacy.
*   **Appearance**: Describes the physical form, such as clear, colorless crystals (pentahydrate) or white crystalline powder (anhydrous), and confirms the absence of foreign matter.
*   **Molar Mass**: Provides the molecular weight (e.g., 248.18 g/mol for pentahydrate; 158.11 g/mol for anhydrous), essential for stoichiometric calculations.
*   **Solubility**: Quantifies its solubility in water at a given temperature, crucial for solution preparation and process design.
*   **pH (of Solution)**: Specifies the pH range of a standard aqueous solution (e.g., 6.5 - 8.5 for a 1% solution), indicating its acidity or alkalinity.
*   **Heavy Metal Content**: Sets strict limits for trace heavy metals (e.g., lead, iron, arsenic), vital for applications in food, pharmaceutical, and environmental sectors.
*   **Insolubles**: Specifies the maximum percentage of insoluble matter, ensuring clarity and preventing process fouling.
*   **Particle Size Distribution**: For specific grades (e.g., photographic grade or those requiring rapid dissolution), this parameter ensures optimal performance.
*   **Moisture Content**: Particularly relevant for anhydrous forms, indicating the maximum allowable water content.

These granular specifications are absolutely crucial for your internal quality control processes, ensuring the product's consistent suitability for your specific industrial processes, analytical applications, or end-product formulations. They form the basis for your incoming goods inspection and vendor qualification.

## 3. Certificate of Analysis (COA): Batch-Specific Quality Assurance

A **Certificate of Analysis (COA)** is a cornerstone document in chemical procurement. It is a batch-specific report that provides irrefutable evidence of the quality and purity of the exact Sodium Thiosulphate you receive. Each COA is generated from rigorous, independent laboratory testing of the actual production batch and provides verifiable, quantitative data against the product specifications [3].

### What a COA Typically Includes and Why It's Important:

*   **Product Name and Grade**: Confirms the specific product and its intended application grade.
*   **Batch/Lot Number**: A unique identifier for traceability, linking the product to its manufacturing history.
*   **Manufacturing Date**: Indicates when the batch was produced.
*   **Expiration/Retest Date**: Provides guidance on the product's shelf life and when it should be re-evaluated.
*   **Test Results**: This is the core of the COA, presenting the actual analytical values for assay, pH, heavy metals, insolubles, and other specified parameters. These results are compared against the defined specifications (e.g., "≥99.0%" for assay).
*   **Testing Methods**: References to the analytical standards and methodologies used (e.g., ASTM, ISO, USP, Ph. Eur.), ensuring reproducibility and comparability of results.
*   **Signature of Authorized Personnel**: Validates the document's authenticity.

Reviewing the COA is a critical, non-negotiable step in your incoming quality inspection process. It provides the assurance that the delivered product meets the agreed-upon standards, minimizing risks of off-spec material impacting your production or end-product quality.

## 5. Material Safety Data Sheet (MSDS) / Safety Data Sheet (SDS): Your Safety Imperative

The **Material Safety Data Sheet (MSDS)**, now universally referred to as **Safety Data Sheet (SDS)** under the Globally Harmonized System (GHS) of Classification and Labelling of Chemicals, is a legally required document. It provides critical health, safety, and environmental information about Sodium Thiosulphate, serving as the cornerstone for ensuring workplace safety, emergency preparedness, and regulatory compliance [5].

### Key Sections of an SDS and Their Importance:

*   **Section 1: Identification**: Product identifier, recommended uses, and supplier contact details. Essential for immediate identification.
*   **Section 2: Hazard(s) Identification**: Classification of hazards (e.g., skin irritant, eye irritant), pictograms, signal words (e.g., "Warning"), and hazard statements. This section provides a quick overview of potential risks.
*   **Section 3: Composition/Information on Ingredients**: Chemical identity and concentration of the substance. Crucial for understanding the exact chemical makeup.
*   **Section 4: First-Aid Measures**: Recommended first aid procedures for various exposure routes (inhalation, skin, eye, ingestion). Vital for immediate response in emergencies.
*   **Section 5: Fire-Fighting Measures**: Suitable extinguishing media, specific hazards arising from the chemical, and protective equipment for firefighters. Ensures effective and safe fire suppression.
*   **Section 6: Accidental Release Measures**: Emergency procedures, containment methods, and cleanup protocols for spills or leaks. Essential for environmental protection and minimizing exposure.
*   **Section 7: Handling and Storage**: Safe handling practices, conditions for safe storage, and incompatibilities. Prevents accidents and maintains product integrity.
*   **Section 8: Exposure Controls/Personal Protection**: Occupational exposure limits (OELs), engineering controls (e.g., ventilation), and recommended Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) such as gloves, eye protection, and respirators. Ensures worker safety.
*   **Section 9: Physical and Chemical Properties**: Appearance, odor, pH, melting point, boiling point, flash point, solubility, and other physical characteristics. Provides fundamental data for risk assessment and process design.
*   **Section 10: Stability and Reactivity**: Chemical stability, possibility of hazardous reactions, conditions to avoid, and incompatible materials. Prevents dangerous chemical interactions.
*   **Section 11: Toxicological Information**: Routes of exposure, symptoms related to physical, chemical, and toxicological characteristics, and acute/chronic health effects. Informs health risk assessments.
*   **Section 12: Ecological Information**: Environmental fate and effects, including ecotoxicity data. Crucial for environmental risk assessment and waste management.
*   **Section 13: Disposal Considerations**: Waste treatment methods and disposal instructions. Ensures compliant and environmentally sound waste management.
*   **Section 14: Transport Information**: UN number, proper shipping name, hazard class, packing group, and environmental hazards for safe transportation.
*   **Section 15: Regulatory Information**: Applicable safety, health, and environmental regulations specific to the product. Ensures legal compliance.

Always consult the SDS before handling Sodium Thiosulphate to fully understand potential risks and implement appropriate safety measures. This document is a living resource and should be reviewed regularly.

## 6. Regulatory Compliance Guides: Navigating the Global Landscape

In today's complex global market, ensuring regulatory compliance for chemical products is a significant challenge. Our Buyer's Resource Center provides access to guides and information that help you navigate the intricate web of international and regional regulations applicable to Sodium Thiosulphate [6].

### Key Regulatory Frameworks to Consider:

*   **REACH (Registration, Evaluation, Authorisation and Restriction of Chemicals) (EU)**: Essential for any company importing or using Sodium Thiosulphate within the European Union. Our guides clarify registration status, authorized uses, and any restrictions.
*   **FDA (U.S. Food and Drug Administration)**: Critical for food-grade or pharmaceutical-grade Sodium Thiosulphate applications in the United States, covering regulations for food additives, drugs, and medical devices.
*   **TSCA (Toxic Substances Control Act) (USA)**: Governs the manufacturing, processing, distribution, use, and disposal of chemical substances in the USA.
*   **National Environmental Protection Agencies**: Compliance with local environmental regulations regarding its use in water treatment and subsequent discharge limits.
*   **Food Additive Regulations**: Specific national or international standards (e.g., JECFA, Codex Alimentarius) for its use as a food additive in various countries.
*   **Transport Regulations (IMDG, IATA, ADR)**: Guidelines for the safe transport of chemicals by sea, air, and road, respectively.

Understanding and adhering to these regulations is not just about legal obligation; it's about responsible business practice, market access, and avoiding costly penalties.
