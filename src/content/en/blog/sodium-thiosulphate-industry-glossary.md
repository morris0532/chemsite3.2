Navigating the chemical industry, especially when dealing with specialized compounds like **Sodium Thiosulphate** (Na₂S₂O₃), often requires a firm grasp of specific terminology. From chemical properties and various forms to diverse applications and regulatory compliance, a clear understanding of industry jargon is crucial for effective communication, informed decision-making, and successful procurement. This **Sodium Thiosulphate Glossary** serves as an essential reference for buyers, researchers, and professionals seeking to demystify the language of this versatile chemical.

This comprehensive guide defines over 80 key terms, ensuring that you have the knowledge needed to confidently engage with suppliers, understand technical specifications, and optimize your operations.

## Key Terms & Definitions for Sodium Thiosulphate

Below is a quick reference table for some of the most frequently encountered terms related to Sodium Thiosulphate.

| Term | Definition | Relevance to Na₂S₂O₃ |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Anhydrous** | A substance containing no water of crystallization. | Refers to Sodium Thiosulphate without water molecules (Na₂S₂O₃). |
| **Pentahydrate** | A substance containing five water molecules of crystallization. | The most common form of Sodium Thiosulphate (Na₂S₂O₃·5H₂O). |
| **Dechlorination** | The process of removing chlorine from water. | Na₂S₂O₃ is a primary agent for this in water treatment. |
| **Iodometry** | A volumetric analytical method using iodine. | Na₂S₂O₃ is a standard titrant in iodometric titrations. |
| **Reducing Agent** | A substance that donates electrons in a redox reaction. | Na₂S₂O₃ acts as a reducing agent in many applications. |
| **Fixer (Photography)** | A chemical solution used to make photographic images permanent. | Na₂S₂O₃ (hypo) dissolves unexposed silver halides. |
| **Antichlor** | A substance used to remove excess chlorine from bleached materials. | Na₂S₂O₃ is used as an antichlor in the textile industry. |
| **Chelating Agent** | A chemical compound that forms soluble complexes with metal ions. | Na₂S₂O₃ can chelate certain heavy metal ions. |

## A-Z Glossary of Sodium Thiosulphate Terminology

### A
*   **Anhydrous**: Refers to Sodium Thiosulphate in its pure form, without any water of crystallization (Na₂S₂O₃). It typically appears as a white, crystalline powder and offers higher assay content compared to hydrated forms.
*   **Antichlor**: A chemical agent, such as Sodium Thiosulphate, used to remove residual chlorine from bleached materials (e.g., textiles, paper pulp) to prevent damage or discoloration.
*   **Aquaculture**: The farming of aquatic organisms. Sodium Thiosulphate is used in aquaculture to dechlorinate water for fish tanks and ponds.
*   **Assay**: The quantitative determination of a specific component in a sample. For Sodium Thiosulphate, assay refers to its purity percentage.

### B
*   **Bleaching**: A process that removes color from textiles or paper pulp. Sodium Thiosulphate is used as an antichlor after bleaching with chlorine-based agents.
*   **BOD (Biochemical Oxygen Demand)**: A measure of the amount of dissolved oxygen consumed by microorganisms in the decomposition of organic matter. High concentrations of thiosulphate can contribute to BOD if discharged untreated.
*   **Bulk Density**: The mass of a powder or granular material divided by the volume it occupies, including the air between particles. Important for storage and transportation.
*   **Buyer's Resource Center**: A centralized hub providing information, tools, and resources for procurement professionals, often including product specifications, market reports, and regulatory guidance.

### C
*   **CAS Number (Chemical Abstracts Service Registry Number)**: A unique numerical identifier for chemical substances. Sodium Thiosulphate Pentahydrate: 10102-17-7; Anhydrous: 7772-98-7.
*   **CFR (Cost and Freight)**: An Incoterm where the seller pays for the carriage of goods to the named port of destination, but risk transfers to the buyer once goods are on board the vessel at the port of shipment.
*   **Chelating Agent**: A chemical compound that forms stable, water-soluble complexes with metal ions, often used to remove heavy metals from solutions.
*   **Chemical Formula**: A way of expressing information about the proportions of atoms that constitute a particular chemical compound. For Sodium Thiosulphate, it is Na₂S₂O₃.
*   **COA (Certificate of Analysis)**: A document issued by a manufacturer or supplier confirming that a product meets its specified quality standards and chemical properties.
*   **COD (Chemical Oxygen Demand)**: A measure of the oxygen equivalent of the organic matter in a water sample that is susceptible to oxidation by a strong chemical oxidant. Thiosulphate can contribute to COD.
*   **Corrosion Inhibitor**: A substance that, when added to a liquid or gas, decreases the corrosion rate of a material, typically a metal or an alloy. Sodium Thiosulphate can act as a mild corrosion inhibitor in some systems.
*   **Crystallization**: The process by which solid crystals precipitate from a solution, melt, or more rarely deposited directly from a gas. Sodium Thiosulphate is commonly supplied in crystalline form.
*   **Cyanide Detoxification**: The process of neutralizing cyanide. Sodium Thiosulphate is used as an antidote for cyanide poisoning and in industrial cyanide destruction processes (e.g., gold mining).

### D
*   **Dechlorination**: The removal of chlorine from water or wastewater. Sodium Thiosulphate is a common dechlorinating agent.
*   **Desulfurization**: The removal of sulfur or sulfur compounds from a material. Sodium Thiosulphate can be involved in some desulfurization processes.
*   **Disinfectant**: A chemical agent used to destroy or inhibit the growth of harmful microorganisms. While not a primary disinfectant, Na₂S₂O₃ neutralizes chlorine, which is a disinfectant.
*   **Dissolved Oxygen (DO)**: The amount of oxygen dissolved in water. Thiosulphate oxidation consumes DO, which can be an environmental concern if not managed.

### E
*   **E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness)**: A Google search quality guideline emphasizing the importance of credible and high-quality content, particularly in YMYL (Your Money Your Life) topics like chemical procurement.
*   **Effluent**: Liquid waste or sewage discharged into a river or the sea. Industrial effluents containing Sodium Thiosulphate must be treated before discharge.
*   **Electrolyte**: A substance that produces an electrically conducting solution when dissolved in a polar solvent, such as water. Sodium Thiosulphate can be used in some electrochemical applications.
*   **Environmental Impact**: The effect of a substance or activity on the living and non-living natural environment. Responsible management of Na₂S₂O₃ minimizes negative environmental impact.
*   **Export Documentation**: Papers required for goods to leave a country, including commercial invoice, packing list, bill of lading, and certificates.

### F
*   **F-Pattern Layout**: A web design principle where users scan content in an 'F' shape, emphasizing the importance of key information at the top and left of a page. Applied in article structuring.
*   **FDA (Food and Drug Administration)**: A U.S. federal agency responsible for protecting public health. Relevant for pharmaceutical or food-grade Sodium Thiosulphate.
*   **Fertilizer**: A chemical or natural substance added to soil or land to increase its fertility. Sodium Thiosulphate can be used as a sulfur-containing fertilizer.
*   **FOB (Free On Board)**: An Incoterm where the seller delivers goods on board the vessel nominated by the buyer at the named port of shipment, with risk transferring at that point.
*   **Food Grade**: A classification indicating that a substance is safe for use in food products or in contact with food, meeting specific purity standards.
*   **Formula Weight**: The sum of the atomic weights of all atoms in a chemical formula. For Na₂S₂O₃, it is 158.11 g/mol (anhydrous) or 248.18 g/mol (pentahydrate).

### G
*   **GHS (Globally Harmonized System of Classification and Labelling of Chemicals)**: An international system for standardizing chemical hazard communication.
*   **Green Chemistry**: The design of chemical products and processes that reduce or eliminate the use or generation of hazardous substances. Na₂S₂O₃ is considered a greener alternative in some applications.

### H
*   **Handling**: Procedures and precautions for the safe manipulation of chemicals. Proper handling of Sodium Thiosulphate is essential to prevent exposure and spills.
*   **Hazardous Waste**: Waste that poses substantial or potential threats to public health or the environment. While Na₂S₂O₃ is generally not hazardous, its mixtures or high concentrations may be.
*   **Heavy Metals**: Metallic elements that have a relatively high density and are toxic or poisonous at low concentrations. Na₂S₂O₃ can be used to chelate or precipitate some heavy metals.
*   **HS Code (Harmonized System Code)**: An internationally standardized system of names and numbers for classifying traded products. Sodium Thiosulphate typically falls under HS Code 2832.30.0000.
*   **Hydrated**: A substance containing water molecules chemically bound within its crystal structure. Sodium Thiosulphate Pentahydrate is a hydrated form.
*   **Hydroponics**: A method of growing plants without soil, using mineral nutrient solutions dissolved in water. Na₂S₂O₃ can be used to adjust water chemistry.
*   **Hypo**: A historical and informal term for Sodium Thiosulphate, particularly in photography.

### I
*   **Incoterms®**: International Commercial Terms, a series of pre-defined commercial terms published by the International Chamber of Commerce (ICC) relating to international commercial law.
*   **Industrial Grade**: A classification indicating a chemical's purity level is suitable for industrial applications, which may be less stringent than food or pharmaceutical grades.
*   **Inorganic Compound**: A chemical compound that does not contain carbon-hydrogen bonds. Sodium Thiosulphate is an inorganic compound.
*   **Iodine**: A chemical element (I₂) that reacts with Sodium Thiosulphate in iodometric titrations.
*   **Iodometry**: A volumetric analytical method that involves the titration of iodine with a reducing agent, typically Sodium Thiosulphate, to determine the concentration of an oxidizing agent.

### L
*   **Leather Tanning**: The process of treating animal hides to produce leather. Sodium Thiosulphate can be used in the tanning process.
*   **Logistics**: The detailed organization and implementation of a complex operation, particularly the movement of equipment, facilities, and personnel. Crucial for chemical supply chains.

### M
*   **Market Analysis**: The study of the attractiveness and dynamics of a special market within a special industry. Includes market size, growth, trends, and competitive landscape.
*   **MSDS (Material Safety Data Sheet)**: A document providing comprehensive information about a hazardous substance, including its properties, hazards, handling, storage, and emergency procedures. Now largely replaced by SDS.

### N
*   **Neutralization**: The process of making a solution neutral (pH 7). Sodium Thiosulphate can neutralize oxidizing agents like chlorine.

### O
*   **Oil & Gas Industry**: Sector involved in the exploration, extraction, refining, transporting, and marketing of petroleum products. Na₂S₂O₃ is used as an oxygen scavenger and in drilling fluids.
*   **Oxidation**: A chemical reaction that involves the loss of electrons or an increase in oxidation state. Sodium Thiosulphate is oxidized when it acts as a reducing agent.
*   **Oxidizing Agent**: A substance that gains electrons and is reduced in a redox reaction. Sodium Thiosulphate reacts with many oxidizing agents.
*   **Oxygen Scavenger**: A substance used to remove dissolved oxygen from water or other liquids, preventing corrosion or degradation. Na₂S₂O₃ is an effective oxygen scavenger.

### P
*   **Packaging**: The materials used to wrap or protect goods. Sodium Thiosulphate is typically packaged in 25kg bags, 1000kg jumbo bags, or drums.
*   **Pentahydrate**: The most common hydrated form of Sodium Thiosulphate, containing five molecules of water of crystallization (Na₂S₂O₃·5H₂O). It appears as clear, colorless crystals and is more stable in air than the anhydrous form.
*   **Pharmaceutical Grade**: A classification indicating a chemical's purity level is suitable for pharmaceutical applications, meeting strict pharmacopoeial standards.
*   **Photography**: The art or process of producing images by the action of radiant energy, especially light, on a sensitive surface. Sodium Thiosulphate is a key component of photographic fixers.
*   **Purity**: The degree to which a substance is free from contamination. Typically expressed as a percentage (e.g., 99% min).

### R
*   **REACH (Registration, Evaluation, Authorisation and Restriction of Chemicals)**: A European Union regulation addressing the production and use of chemical substances, and their potential impacts on both human health and the environment.
*   **Reducing Agent**: A substance that causes another substance to gain electrons (be reduced) while itself losing electrons (being oxidized). Sodium Thiosulphate is a strong reducing agent.
*   **Reduction**: A chemical reaction that involves the gain of electrons or a decrease in oxidation state.
*   **Regulatory Compliance**: Adherence to laws, regulations, guidelines, and specifications relevant to a business process. Essential for chemical manufacturing and trade.
*   **Risk Transfer**: The point in a trade transaction where the responsibility for loss or damage to goods shifts from the seller to the buyer, as defined by Incoterms.

### S
*   **Safety Data Sheet (SDS)**: A document providing comprehensive information about a hazardous substance, including its properties, hazards, handling, storage, and emergency procedures. Replaced MSDS.
*   **Shelf Life**: The length of time for which an item remains suitable for use, sale, or consumption. Important for chemical storage.
*   **Shipping**: The process of transporting goods from one place to another. International shipping of chemicals requires specialized knowledge and documentation.
*   **Sinopeakchem**: A hypothetical chemical supplier, often used as an example in industry guides.
*   **Slug**: A URL-friendly version of a title, typically lowercase and with spaces replaced by hyphens. Used for SEO-friendly URLs.
*   **Sodium Thiosulphate (Na₂S₂O₃)**: The chemical compound that is the subject of this glossary. Also known as Sodium Thiosulfate or "hypo."
*   **Solubility**: The maximum amount of a substance that can dissolve in a given amount of solvent at a specific temperature. Sodium Thiosulphate is highly soluble in water.
*   **Storage**: Conditions and methods for safely keeping chemicals. Proper storage prevents degradation and hazards.
*   **Supply Chain**: The entire process of making and selling commercial goods, including every stage from the supply of materials and the manufacture of the goods through to their distribution and sale.
*   **Sustainability**: The ability to be maintained at a certain rate or level. In chemistry, it refers to practices that minimize environmental impact and resource depletion.

### T
*   **TDS (Technical Data Sheet)**: A document providing detailed technical specifications and properties of a product.
*   **Textile Industry**: The industry involved in the production of yarn, cloth, and clothing. Sodium Thiosulphate is used as an antichlor in textile processing.
*   **Titration**: A common laboratory method of quantitative chemical analysis to determine the concentration of an identified analyte. Iodometric titration uses Na₂S₂O₃.
*   **Trade Terms**: Standardized clauses in international sales contracts that define the responsibilities of buyers and sellers for the delivery of goods (e.g., Incoterms).

### W
*   **Waste Disposal**: The process of discarding unwanted materials. Proper waste disposal of Sodium Thiosulphate is crucial for environmental protection.
*   **Water Treatment**: Any process that improves the quality of water to make it more acceptable for a specific end-use. Dechlorination with Na₂S₂O₃ is a key step.
*   **Wastewater**: Water that has been used in homes, businesses, or as part of an industrial process. Often requires treatment before discharge.
