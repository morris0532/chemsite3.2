## How to Choose a Reliable Oxalic Acid Supplier in China

China is the global powerhouse for [oxalic acid](https://www.sinopeakchem.com/en/blog/what-is-oxalic-acid) production, accounting for more than 70% of the world's total capacity. For international buyers, sourcing from China offers significant cost advantages and supply stability. However, the vast number of manufacturers and exporters can make it challenging to identify a truly reliable partner.

This guide outlines the critical factors you must consider when evaluating an oxalic acid supplier in China to ensure product quality, regulatory compliance, and smooth logistics.

### Why Source Oxalic Acid from China?

The dominance of Chinese oxalic acid in the global market is driven by several key factors:

*   **Cost Efficiency**: Advanced production technologies and economies of scale allow Chinese producers to offer prices that are often 30-50% lower than those in Europe or North America.
*   **Massive Capacity**: With an annual production capacity exceeding 1 million tons, China can easily accommodate large-scale industrial orders.
*   **Integrated Supply Chain**: From raw materials like carbon monoxide and sodium hydroxide to specialized packaging and global logistics, China provides a one-stop solution.
*   **Export Expertise**: Leading Chinese chemical exporters have decades of experience navigating international trade regulations and shipping requirements.

### 7 Key Factors When Evaluating a Supplier

To find a partner that meets your industrial standards, evaluate them against these seven criteria:

#### 1. Production Capacity & Supply Consistency
Ensure the supplier can meet your current and future volume requirements. Ask about their annual output and their raw material sourcing strategies. A reliable supplier should maintain consistent stock levels even during peak seasons or environmental inspection periods.

#### 2. Quality Certifications
Verify that the supplier adheres to international quality management standards. Essential certifications include:
*   **ISO 9001**: For quality management systems.
*   **REACH Registration**: Mandatory for exporting to the European Union.
*   **SGS/BV Verification**: Third-party audits that confirm the legitimacy of the factory and its products.

#### 3. Product Purity & COA (Certificate of Analysis)
Industrial-grade oxalic acid typically requires a purity of at least 99.6%. Always request a [COA (Certificate of Analysis)](https://www.sinopeakchem.com/en/blog/oxalic-acid-comprehensive-guide) for every batch. The COA should clearly state the levels of purity, moisture, and heavy metal impurities (such as Iron, Chloride, and Sulfate).

#### 4. Packaging Options & Customization
Proper [packaging](https://www.sinopeakchem.com/en/blog/oxalic-acid-industrial-applications) is vital for maintaining product quality during long-distance sea freight. Look for suppliers offering:
*   Standard 25kg or 50kg PP/PE bags.
*   1000kg jumbo bags for bulk industrial use.
*   Custom labeling and palletizing services to meet your local warehouse requirements.

#### 5. Export Experience & Documentation
A reliable exporter should be familiar with the documentation required for your specific country. This includes Commercial Invoices, Packing Lists, Bills of Lading, Certificates of Origin, and comprehensive MSDS (Material Safety Data Sheets).

#### 6. Communication & Response Time
Effective communication is the foundation of a successful international partnership. Your supplier should have a dedicated English-speaking team that responds to inquiries within 24 hours and provides regular updates on order status.

#### 7. After-Sales Support
What happens if there is a quality discrepancy or a shipping delay? Choose a supplier with a clear claim handling process and a commitment to technical support.

### Red Flags to Watch Out For

Be cautious if you encounter any of the following:
*   **Prices far below market average**: This often indicates diluted purity or the presence of excessive impurities.
*   **Refusal of third-party inspections**: Legitimate suppliers should welcome inspections by SGS or Intertek.
*   **Lack of physical factory evidence**: Always ask for recent photos or videos of the production line and warehouse.
*   **Unverifiable company information**: Check the supplier's registration on official Chinese government portals or reputable B2B platforms.

### How to Verify a Chinese Supplier

Follow these steps for a secure sourcing process:
1.  **Online Research**: Check their presence on platforms like Alibaba, Made-in-China, and their official website.
2.  **Request Samples**: Always test a small sample before placing a bulk order.
3.  **Third-Party Testing**: Send the sample to an independent lab to verify the COA data.
4.  **Small Trial Order**: Start with a 1-5 ton trial order to evaluate their logistics and communication.
5.  **Factory Audit**: If possible, arrange a virtual or physical factory tour.
