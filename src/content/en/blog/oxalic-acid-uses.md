## Top 15 Industrial Uses of Oxalic Acid You Should Know

Oxalic acid, chemically known as ethanedioic acid, is the simplest dicarboxylic acid and one of the most versatile organic acids widely used across numerous industries. Its unique chemical properties—including strong acidity, chelating ability, and reducing power—make it indispensable in applications ranging from metal treatment and textile dyeing to rare earth extraction and pharmaceutical synthesis.

This comprehensive guide delves into the top 15 industrial uses of oxalic acid, highlighting its mechanisms and significance in each sector.

### 1. Rust Removal & Metal Cleaning

Oxalic acid is highly effective in removing rust (iron oxides) from metal surfaces. It reacts with insoluble iron oxides to form soluble iron oxalate complexes, which can then be easily rinsed away. This makes it a preferred choice for cleaning steel parts, pipes, and tools, as it is generally milder than stronger mineral acids like hydrochloric acid, reducing the risk of excessive base metal corrosion. A typical concentration for rust removal is a 5-10% aqueous solution.

### 2. Wood Bleaching & Restoration

For wood restoration, oxalic acid is invaluable. It effectively removes iron stains, water spots, and graying caused by weathering, restoring the wood's natural color without damaging its fibers. It's commonly used on furniture, decks, and antique wood. A 12-15% hot water solution is often recommended for optimal results.

### 3. Textile Dyeing & Bleaching

In the textile industry, oxalic acid serves multiple functions. It acts as a mordant, helping certain dyes bind to fibers and improving colorfastness. It's also used as a bleaching agent for natural fibers like cotton and linen, and as a reducing agent in printing processes to prevent discoloration. Its pH-regulating properties are crucial in various stages of textile processing.

### 4. Leather Tanning & Processing

Oxalic acid plays a role in leather processing, particularly in the deliming stage, where it can be used as an alternative to sulfuric acid. It helps adjust pH, bleaches the leather, and contributes to improving the softness and color uniformity of the final product.

### 5. Marble & Stone Polishing

For marble and other stone surfaces, oxalic acid is used as a polishing agent. It reacts with calcium carbonate in the stone to form calcium oxalate, a hard, insoluble salt that creates a protective, glossy layer, enhancing the stone's shine and removing minor stains and rust spots.

### 6. Ceramic & Tile Manufacturing

In ceramic and tile production, oxalic acid is incorporated into glaze formulations as a fluxing agent, helping to lower melting points and improve the flow of glazes. It also aids in cleaning and treating tile surfaces and can enhance the whiteness of ceramic products.

### 7. Rare Earth Extraction

Oxalic acid is a critical reagent in the separation and purification of rare earth elements. The rare earth oxalate precipitation method utilizes oxalic acid to selectively precipitate rare earth ions from leachate solutions as insoluble oxalates, which are then calcined to form rare earth oxides. This method is widely employed in the rare earth industry, particularly in China.

### 8. Wastewater Treatment

Due to its chelating properties, oxalic acid is used in wastewater treatment to remove heavy metal ions by forming stable, insoluble complexes that can be easily separated. It can also be used in conjunction with Fenton reactions to degrade organic pollutants and adjust the Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD) in industrial effluents.

### 9. Pharmaceutical Industry

In the pharmaceutical sector, oxalic acid serves as an important intermediate in the synthesis of various drugs, including antibiotics like tetracycline and oxytetracycline, as well as phenobarbital and borneol. Its high purity grades are essential for ensuring the safety and efficacy of pharmaceutical products.

### 10. Aluminium Anodizing

Oxalic acid is used as an electrolyte in the anodizing of aluminum. The oxalic acid anodizing process produces a hard, corrosion-resistant, and aesthetically pleasing oxide layer on aluminum surfaces, often resulting in a yellowish tint. This method is favored for applications requiring enhanced wear resistance and decorative finishes, such as in aerospace and architectural components.

### 11. Beekeeping (Varroa Mite Treatment)

Oxalic acid is a common organic treatment for Varroa destructor mites in honeybee colonies. It can be applied through vaporization, dribble, or spray methods. When applied correctly, it is highly effective against mites on adult bees with minimal harm to the bees themselves, making it a popular choice for organic beekeeping.

### 12. Electroplating Industry

In electroplating, oxalic acid can function as a complexing agent or a pH regulator in plating baths. It helps to achieve smooth and uniform plating layers, particularly in the electrolytic polishing of metals like aluminum and copper, by controlling the metal ion concentration and bath acidity.

### 13. Cleaning Agents & Polishes

Beyond rust removal, oxalic acid is a component in various household and industrial cleaning agents and polishes. It's used in formulations for cleaning boat hulls, removing mineral deposits, and polishing chrome and stainless steel, thanks to its acidic and chelating properties.

### 14. Chemical Synthesis

As a fundamental organic acid, oxalic acid is a versatile building block in numerous chemical syntheses. It is used in the production of various esters, amides, and other organic compounds, serving as a precursor for many specialty chemicals and polymers.

### 15. Photography & Printing

Historically, oxalic acid has found applications in photography as a component in some developing solutions and as a bleaching agent. In printing, it can be used in certain processes for pH adjustment and as a reducing agent.

### Conclusion

Oxalic acid's broad spectrum of industrial applications underscores its importance as a fundamental chemical compound. From traditional uses in textiles and metal treatment to critical roles in emerging sectors like rare earth extraction and advanced materials, its unique properties continue to drive innovation and efficiency across diverse global industries. Understanding these applications is key to appreciating the full value and versatility of this essential organic acid.
