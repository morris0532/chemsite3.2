Maintaining optimal water quality is the cornerstone of success for both professional aquarists and swimming pool operators. While chlorine and chloramines are essential for disinfecting municipal water supplies, they are highly toxic to aquatic life and can be irritating to human skin and eyes at high concentrations. **Sodium Thiosulphate (Na₂S₂O₃)** is the industry-standard solution for rapid, safe, and cost-effective dechlorination. This guide explores its application in aquatic environments and provides essential dosage and safety information.

## 1. Why Dechlorination is Essential

### 1.1 In Aquariums and Aquaculture
Fish and invertebrates "breathe" by passing water over their gills. Chlorine is a strong oxidant that causes immediate damage to these delicate tissues, leading to respiratory distress, suppressed immune systems, and often rapid mortality. Even trace amounts of chlorine can destroy the beneficial nitrifying bacteria in a biological filter, causing a catastrophic "ammonia spike."

### 1.2 In Swimming Pools and Spas
While chlorine is necessary for sanitation, "over-chlorination" can occur after a "shock" treatment or due to dosing errors. High chlorine levels make the water uncomfortable for swimmers and can damage pool liners and filtration equipment. Sodium Thiosulphate allows operators to quickly bring chlorine levels back down to a safe and comfortable range (typically 1-3 ppm).

---

## 2. The Mechanism of Action

Sodium Thiosulphate works by chemically reducing free chlorine (hypochlorite) into harmless chloride ions (the same ions found in common table salt).

**The Chemical Reaction:**
**Na₂S₂O₃ + 4HOCl + H₂O → 2NaHSO₄ + 4HCl**

The reaction is nearly instantaneous, making it ideal for "on-the-fly" water conditioning during water changes or pool maintenance.

---

## 3. Dosage and Application Guide

### 3.1 For Aquariums
For standard municipal water containing 1-2 ppm of chlorine, a very small amount of Sodium Thiosulphate is required.
*   **Standard Dosage**: Approximately **0.1 grams per 100 liters** of water is usually sufficient to neutralize typical chlorine levels.
*   **Pro Tip**: Many hobbyists create a "stock solution" by dissolving 50 grams of Sodium Thiosulphate in 1 liter of distilled water. Adding 1-2 drops of this solution per gallon of tap water provides a safe and effective dechlorinator.

### 3.2 For Swimming Pools
To lower chlorine levels in a pool, the dosage depends on the volume of the pool and the amount of chlorine to be removed.
*   **General Rule**: To reduce chlorine by 1 ppm in a 10,000-gallon pool, approximately **2.5 ounces (70 grams)** of Sodium Thiosulphate is required.
*   **Application**: Always pre-dissolve the crystals in a bucket of water and distribute the solution evenly around the perimeter of the pool while the pump is running.

---

## 4. Safety and Environmental Considerations

*   **Non-Toxic to Fish**: When used at recommended dosages, Sodium Thiosulphate is remarkably safe for fish, plants, and invertebrates. It does not affect the pH of the water significantly in well-buffered systems.
*   **Oxygen Levels**: In extremely high doses, Sodium Thiosulphate can slightly reduce dissolved oxygen levels. In heavily stocked aquariums, ensure good aeration during treatment.
*   **Chloramines**: While Sodium Thiosulphate breaks the chlorine-ammonia bond in chloramines, it only neutralizes the chlorine. The residual ammonia must be handled by a healthy biological filter or an additional ammonia-locking agent.

---

## 5. Quality Standards for Aquatic Use

For both aquariums and pools, it is recommended to use **High-Purity Industrial or Food Grade Sodium Thiosulphate Pentahydrate**:
*   **Purity**: ≥99.0%
*   **Heavy Metals**: <10 ppm (essential for sensitive invertebrates like shrimp and coral)
*   **Appearance**: Clear, colorless crystals that dissolve without residue.
