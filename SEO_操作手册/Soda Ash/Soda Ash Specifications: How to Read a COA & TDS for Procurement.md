## 1. TDS vs. COA: What’s the Difference for a Buyer?
Before placing an order with a **China Soda Ash Supplier**, you must understand the distinction between these two documents.

*   **Technical Data Sheet (TDS)**: A general specification sheet provided by the manufacturer. It outlines the *standard* range of the product. It is a marketing and technical reference.
*   **Certificate of Analysis (COA)**: A batch-specific document. It shows the *actual* test results for the specific lot of Soda Ash you are purchasing. 

### Sourcing Strategy:
Always request a **Batch-Specific COA** before the cargo leaves **Tianjin Port**. A generic TDS is not enough to ensure quality for high-stakes manufacturing.

---

## 2. Decoding the Key Chemical Parameters
The "Big Three" parameters determine the value and application of your Sodium Carbonate.

### A. Total Alkalinity (as Na2CO3)
This is the purity of the product. Standard industrial grade is typically **99.2% min**.
*   **Problem**: Low purity means you are paying for "fillers" and impurities, which can alter the stoichiometry of your chemical reactions.
*   **Solution**: Ensure the COA shows a consistent purity level above 99.2% to maintain process stability.

### B. Iron Content (Fe) – The Glass Maker’s Priority
Iron is the most dangerous impurity in glass manufacturing.
*   **Impact**: Even 0.005% Fe can turn clear glass green. For **Solar Glass**, the requirement is even stricter (≤ 0.0015%).
*   **B2B Intent**: Procurement managers for glass factories should prioritize suppliers who use the **Hou Process** or high-quality natural trona, as these typically yield lower iron content.

### C. Chlorides (as NaCl)
Chlorides are a byproduct of the Solvay process.
*   **Impact**: High chlorides cause stress corrosion cracking in stainless steel equipment.
*   **Target**: For chemical synthesis and metallurgy, aim for chlorides below 0.5% on the COA.

---

## 3. Physical Specifications: Density and Particle Size
Chemical purity is only half the story. The physical "form" of the Soda Ash dictates how it behaves in your factory.

### A. Bulk Density (Dense vs. Light)
*   **Dense**: Target 1.0 - 1.2 g/cm³. If the COA shows a density below 0.9, it may cause "dusting" in your furnace.
*   **Light**: Target 0.5 - 0.6 g/cm³. Essential for rapid dissolution in detergent mixing.

### B. Sieve Analysis (Particle Size Distribution)
A professional COA will include a sieve test.
*   **Why it matters**: For glass manufacturing, the particle size of Soda Ash Dense must match the silica sand (typically 300-1000 microns) to prevent raw material segregation.

---

## 4. How to Spot a "Fake" or Low-Quality COA
In the **Bulk Buy Soda Ash** market, some suppliers may provide "template" COAs that do not reflect the actual cargo.

### Red Flags to Watch For:
1.  **Identical Results**: If the results for Na2CO3, Fe, and NaCl are exactly the same across multiple batches (e.g., always exactly 99.20%, 0.0030%), the COA is likely a template.
2.  **Missing Batch Numbers**: Every COA must link to a specific production lot and packaging date.
3.  **No Lab Stamp**: A valid COA must be signed by the Quality Control (QC) manager and stamped by the factory lab.

---

## 5. Compliance Standards: ISO, REACH, and SGS
A leading **China Soda Ash Factory** will back their COA with international certifications.

*   **ISO 9001:2015**: Ensures the factory has a consistent quality management system.
*   **REACH Registration**: Mandatory for any buyer in the European Union. The COA should mention the REACH registration number.
*   **Third-Party Inspection (SGS/BV)**: For new B2B relationships, we recommend hiring SGS to perform an independent sampling and analysis at the port of loading.
