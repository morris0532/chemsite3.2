This guide provides a comprehensive checklist of the critical KPIs (Key Performance Indicators) for auditing a China Soda Ash factory, ensuring your supply chain is built on a foundation of reliability and transparency.

| Audit Pillar | Critical KPI | Why it Matters for B2B Buyers |

## 1. Quality Control (QC) and Laboratory Capabilities

A leading Soda Ash factory must have a laboratory that is as sophisticated as its production line.

### What to Verify:
*   **Testing Frequency**: Does the factory test every batch (approx. every 2-4 hours) or only once per day? High-frequency testing is essential for maintaining consistent PSD (Particle Size Distribution).
*   **Equipment Calibration**: Ask for the latest calibration certificates for their analytical balances, titrators, and ICP-OES (Inductively Coupled Plasma) machines.
*   **Sample Retention**: Does the factory keep "retention samples" for every shipment for at least 12 months? This is critical for resolving any quality disputes that may arise after the material has arrived at your plant.

---

## 2. Environmental and Safety Compliance (ESG)

In China, environmental regulations are strictly enforced. A factory that is not in full compliance is at risk of sudden "government-mandated shutdowns," which can disrupt your entire production schedule.

### What to Verify:
*   **Environmental Permits**: Ensure the factory has a valid "Pollutant Discharge Permit" and a clean record with the local Bureau of Ecology and Environment.
*   **Safety Management**: Look for **ISO 45001** (Occupational Health and Safety) certification. A safe factory is a stable factory.
*   **REACH Registration**: For European buyers, verify that the manufacturer (or their only representative) has a full **REACH Registration** for Sodium Carbonate.

---

## 3. Production Technology and Capacity

Understanding the factory's technology helps you predict the product's impurity profile and cost structure.

### What to Verify:
*   **Process Type**: Is it a **Solvay** or **Hou (Combined)** process plant? As discussed in our previous guides, the Hou process often yields lower chloride levels and is more resource-efficient.
*   **Maintenance Schedule**: When is the factory's next "annual maintenance shutdown"? Knowing this 6 months in advance allows you to build up your inventory and avoid supply gaps.
*   **Export Experience**: Does the factory have a dedicated export department that understands international shipping documents (BL, COA, PL, Invoice) and port requirements?

---

## 4. Logistics and Loading Quality Control

The journey from the factory gate to the container ship is where many quality issues (like moisture ingress or bag damage) occur.

### What to Verify:
*   **Warehouse Conditions**: Is the Soda Ash stored on pallets in a dry, well-ventilated area? (Refer to our **Storage Best Practices** guide).
*   **Loading Inspection**: Does the factory perform a "Container Cleanliness Inspection" before loading? They should reject any container with holes, rust, or odors.
*   **Packaging Durability**: Inspect the micron thickness of the PE liners and the UV-stabilization of the outer PP bags.

---

## 5. Financial and Credit Standing

In the B2B chemical trade, financial trust is the "grease" that keeps the wheels turning.

### What to Verify:
*   **Sinosure Rating**: Ask if the supplier is rated by **Sinosure (China Export & Credit Insurance Corporation)**. A high rating indicates a financially stable company that is trusted by the Chinese government.
*   **Credit Terms**: For long-term partners, is the supplier willing to offer **OA (Open Account)** or **DP (Documents against Payment)** terms backed by Sinosure? This significantly improves your company's cash flow.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### 1. Can I hire a third party to perform the audit?
Yes. Companies like **SGS, Intertek, or BV** offer professional factory auditing services in China. This is highly recommended for new, high-volume contracts.

### 2. What is the most common "red flag" during a factory audit?
A lack of batch-to-batch consistency in the COA data. If the numbers are too "perfect" or never change, it may indicate that the factory is not performing real-time testing.

### 3. How do I verify a factory's REACH registration?
You can ask for the **REACH Registration Number** and verify it through the ECHA (European Chemicals Agency) database or by contacting the manufacturer's Only Representative (OR).

### 4. Why is the factory's proximity to the port important?
Proximity to **Tianjin or Lianyungang** reduces inland transport costs and minimizes the time the material is exposed to the elements during transit.

### 5. How do China suppliers handle "Social Responsibility" audits?
Many leading manufacturers are now undergoing **Sedex (SMETA)** or **EcoVadis** audits to meet the ESG requirements of global FMCG and glass brands.
