## China Oxalic Acid Manufacturer & Exporter: Quality, Certification & Logistics

China stands as the undisputed leader in global oxalic acid production and export, accounting for over 70% of the world's total capacity [1]. This dominance is not merely due to scale but also a result of advanced manufacturing capabilities, stringent quality control, and a robust export infrastructure. For international buyers, understanding the landscape of Chinese oxalic acid manufacturers and exporters is key to securing a reliable and high-quality supply.

### China's Dominance in Oxalic Acid Production

China's leading position in the oxalic acid market is driven by several factors:

-   **Vast Production Capacity**: With an annual production exceeding 1 million tons, China ensures a consistent and ample supply to meet global demand.
-   **Key Production Hubs**: Major production regions include Shandong, Hebei, Inner Mongolia, and Jiangxi provinces, which benefit from integrated supply chains and favorable industrial policies.
-   **Technological Advancement**: Chinese manufacturers have perfected the oxidation method, utilizing raw materials like starch and molasses to produce high-purity oxalic acid efficiently.
-   **Significant Export Volume**: China exports oxalic acid to over 100 countries, demonstrating extensive experience in international trade and logistics.

### Quality Control Standards in Chinese Factories

Reputable Chinese oxalic acid manufacturers adhere to international quality management systems to ensure product consistency and purity:

-   **ISO 9001 Certification**: This widely recognized standard for quality management systems is a baseline for most leading manufacturers, ensuring systematic control over production processes.
-   **Comprehensive QC Process**: Quality control spans the entire production chain, from raw material inspection to in-process monitoring and final product testing. Each batch typically undergoes rigorous analysis before shipment.
-   **Third-Party Inspections**: Many buyers opt for independent third-party inspections (e.g., SGS, BV) to verify product quality and quantity before loading.
-   **Certificate of Analysis (COA)**: A COA is provided with every shipment, detailing the chemical properties and purity of the specific batch, ensuring transparency and compliance with specifications.

### Key Certifications for International Export

For global trade, various certifications are crucial to meet regulatory requirements and market demands:

-   **REACH Registration**: Essential for exporting oxalic acid to the European Union, ensuring compliance with chemical safety regulations.
-   **FDA Compliance**: Relevant for applications in food processing or pharmaceuticals, where specific purity and safety standards are mandated by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration.
-   **HALAL & KOSHER Certifications**: Important for markets with specific religious dietary laws, indicating that the product meets their purity and processing requirements.
-   **ISO 14001 Environmental Management**: Demonstrates a manufacturer's commitment to environmental protection, often a preferred criterion for environmentally conscious buyers.

### Export Process & Logistics from China

An efficient export process is vital for timely delivery. Chinese manufacturers typically follow a streamlined procedure:

1.  **Order Confirmation**: Finalization of product specifications, quantity, price, and payment terms.
2.  **Production & Quality Control**: Manufacturing and rigorous testing of the oxalic acid batch.
3.  **Packaging**: Products are packed according to international standards, often in 25kg, 50kg bags, or 500kg, 1000kg jumbo bags, with moisture-proof inner liners.
4.  **Customs Clearance**: Preparation and submission of all necessary export documentation to Chinese customs.
5.  **Shipping Arrangement**: Coordination with freight forwarders for ocean, rail, or air transport.

**Main Export Ports**: Shanghai, Qingdao, Tianjin, and Ningbo are primary hubs for chemical exports, offering extensive global shipping routes.

**Export Documents**: A complete set of documents includes Commercial Invoice, Packing List, Bill of Lading, COA, MSDS, and Certificate of Origin. For more details on [shipping costs](/en/blog/chemical-shipping-logistics-china-guide) and logistics, refer to our comprehensive guide.
